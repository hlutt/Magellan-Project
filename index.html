What is a Magellan Project?

Magellans are grants given to students at Washington and Jefferson to complete research for an intended purpose. The information studied and knowledge learned is then created into a project after the trip and research is completed.

My Magellan

The purpose of my Magellan project was to have the opportunity to learn more about some of the Native American tribes that live within the United States. While I have had the opportunity to learn about many different cultures, I felt as if I lacked the knowledge I would like to have about so many groups of people that make up the country I grew up in. Therefore, in visiting these beautiful places, I was able to learn about the places that shaped their culture and learn about the challenges different tribes continue to face to this day in my research.

I have studied many different cultures throughout my 4 years of college. My German major introduced me to the culture of German speaking countries and while completing a semester abroad, I was able to immerse myself but also travel and learn about so many different places. I have also taken Russian classes that introduced me to another part of the world, but no matter how many cultures I have learned about in my classes, I still lack information about many that exist in the same country I live in. With this research grant, I hoped to change that, and I feel like I succeeded in learning a lot more about many different tribes in the areas I was able to visit.
